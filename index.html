Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

As you navigate through the multiple charts available we can observe some intersting facts between
lack of healtcare, smokers and obesity versus poverty rate, median age and median household income:

Let's first compare the poverty rate :
We observe a ligth positive correlation between the poverty rate and the lack a healthcare. This means the more a region is poor the bigger the lack of healthcare.
The observation is the same between the smokers and the poverty rate. This correlation seems a bit stronger than the correlation between poverty and healthcare. The more smokers in a region, the higher the rate of poverty.
We observe the same trend between obesity and poverty rate. The higher the poverty rate the higher the obesity rate. This is the strongest positive correlation observed for the poverty rate.

Let's now compare the median age :
We observe a small negative correlation between the median age and the lack of healthcare. This means that the younger, the less someone subscribes to a healthcare.
The correlation is less visible between the median age and the percent smokers in a region. A small positive correlation
The median age doesn't explain either the obesity rate.

Let's finally compare the household income :
We observe a small negative correlation between the median income and the lack of healthcare. This means that the less income, the less someone subscribes to a healthcare.
The negative correlation is much more visible between the median income and the percent smokers in a region.The less people earn, the more they smoke
We observe the same trend between the obesity and the income, the obesity rate is higher where the median income is lower
